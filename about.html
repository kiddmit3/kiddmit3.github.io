About Me

I'm a Software Engineer with a 6+ years background building biometric applications out here in Southern California. I enjoy creating anything that's practical, user-friendly, and visually modern. I love pairing intuitive solutions to problems, avoiding overengineering and aimming for approaches that are clean, practical, and optimized.

Education

I originally pursued a path in Dentistry years ago—completed the full process, took the DATs, and was about to enter a graduate program in Boston. But as the financial and timeline realities became clearer, I decided to pivot. Throughout university, I took computer courses and had already experience in web development, which led me to enroll in UCLA's immersive Full Stack Development Bootcamp.

Realization

I've always been interested in computers and technology—one of my earliest experiences was overclocking (and accidentally melting) my family computers's AMD XP 2400+ CPU (I foolishly tried reaching the coveted 3.0Ghz!) For a long time, I thought programming meant building everything from scratch. But later on, I discovered libraries and frameworks — tools that abstract complexity and let developers focus on stitching solutions together. That changed everything and helped me see software as something both creative and collaborative. That, it takes a team to build something meaningful.
